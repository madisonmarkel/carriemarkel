About Me

My path as a design has not been a typical one, but I credit the success I’ve found to my early career days as a teacher. Turns out, holding the attention of 30 second-grade students for an entire day across multiple subjects teaches you a thing or two about human-centered design. Creating differentiated lesson plans and structuring a classroom environment were my crash course in designing for specific users & journeys, and bringing it all back together into a cohesive experience.

I’ve since shifted my focus to digital design but I still value solving meaningful problems with a focus on web accessibility. I’m currently working in govtech and mulling over the future of service design for cities and governments.
